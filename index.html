Digital Bill of Rights

  • Right to Transparency
  • Right to Access, Correction, and Deletion
  • Right to Consent and Opt-In Monetization
  • Right to Portability
  • Right to Algorithmic Due Process
  • Right to Fair Compensation

What the DDFA Does

Ensures transparency, regulates profiling, enforces strong data security, mandates data broker registration, and establishes economic participation when personal data is monetized.

Why It Matters

Minnesotans deserve digital self-determination. The DDFA protects vulnerable communities, restores trust, reduces exploitation, and ensures that individuals benefit from the value their data creates.

Your Digital Rights Under the DDFA

  • Transparency about what data is collected, where it goes, and why.
  • Access & deletion rights for your personal data.
  • Correction of inaccurate or harmful data.
  • Opt-in only for any data monetization.
  • Fair compensation when your data generates profit.
  • Protection from profiling in housing, work, credit, and services.
  • Algorithmic due process ‚Äì the right to an explanation.
  • Security standards to prevent breaches and misuse.
  • Special safeguards for children and sensitive data.
  • Enforceable rights ‚Äì with real penalties for violations.
